Planning an international aesthetic product shipment

Good logistics starts before a parcel is packed. These are the details professional buyers should clarify early.

SOLA Knowledge Team · Updated June 2026

International procurement involves more than choosing products and agreeing on a price. Destination requirements, product sensitivity, carrier options and communication all affect the outcome.

1. Start with the exact destination

Provide the country, city and postal code when requesting a quote. This helps the supplier evaluate realistic carrier options and delivery expectations. Import requirements differ by market, so professional buyers should independently confirm local eligibility before ordering.

2. Separate product availability from shipping suitability

A product may be available while a particular route or delivery schedule is unsuitable. Ask whether any selected items require special handling and whether the proposed shipping method is appropriate for the order.

Buyer checklist
  • Complete product names and quantities
  • Destination country, city and postal code
  • Preferred delivery window
  • Any known import or documentation requirements
  • A reachable contact for delivery updates

3. Confirm what happens after dispatch

Before payment, understand when tracking is supplied, which carrier may be used and how delivery exceptions are communicated. Tracking is a visibility tool—not a guarantee against customs or carrier delays.

4. Plan around realistic timelines

Avoid scheduling appointments or customer commitments against an unconfirmed arrival date. International transit can be affected by weekends, public holidays, customs review and last-mile conditions.

5. Inspect promptly on arrival

Document the parcel condition and verify the contents soon after delivery. If something appears unusual, retain packaging and contact the supplier with clear photographs and order details.
